# Skill: Company Research

**Description**: Guidelines for gathering context on the target company.

## Step 1: Classify the Company Type

Before collecting any other data, determine what kind of company is posting the job:

| Type | Signals | Cover Letter Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct Employer** (product/service company) | Career page on their own domain, "our product/platform", named tech stack, internal team language | Standard AIDA — reference their mission, product, or tech |
| **IT Contractor / Consulting Firm** | "work with our clients", "project-based", "various industries", client portfolio page | See → [IT Contractor Opening](#it-contractor-opening) |
| **Recruiting Agency / Staffing** | "we are recruiting on behalf of", "our client", generic role description, agency domain (e.g. hays.de, michael-page.com) | See → [Recruiting Agency Opening](#recruiting-agency-opening) |

---

## IT Contractor Opening

When the employer is an **IT consulting or contracting firm**, they don't have a single product to reference. What they sell is *reliable, skilled engineers who deliver for clients*. Your opening must speak directly to that value proposition.

**Strategy**: Lead with your track record of delivering across diverse client environments, and signal that you thrive in the project-based, context-switching nature of consulting work.

**Opening formula**:
> [Specific domain/tech strength] + [evidence of cross-client or cross-domain delivery] + [why the consulting/contractor model suits you]

**Example (English)**:
> "Over [X] years at [previous employer], I delivered full-stack solutions across healthcare, logistics, and enterprise SaaS clients — each with its own tech stack and timeline pressure. That breadth is exactly what consulting demands, and it's the environment where I do my best work."

**Example (German)**:
> „[X] Jahre lang habe ich bei [vorheriger Arbeitgeber] Fullstack-Lösungen für Kunden aus dem Gesundheitswesen, der Logistik und dem Enterprise-Bereich entwickelt – jedes Projekt mit eigenem Tech-Stack und eigenen Anforderungen. Genau diese Vielseitigkeit ist es, die mich an projektbasierter Arbeit reizt."

**Key rules**:
- Do NOT reference "your product" or "your mission" — they don't have one to reference.
- DO reference your adaptability, delivery speed, and client-facing professionalism.
- If the firm has a named specialty (e.g., SAP consulting, cloud transformation), reference that niche specifically.

## Recruiting Agency Opening

When a **recruiting agency** is placing you at a client company, they need to trust you as a candidate they can confidently present. Your opening should signal that you are low-risk, high-quality, and easy to place.

**Strategy**: Lead with a crisp professional identity statement — the recruiter needs to instantly understand your profile and see you as placeable for the role they described.

**Opening formula**:
> [Clear role identity] + [years + domain] + [what makes you stand out as a candidate]

**Example (English)**:
> "I'm a fullstack engineer with [X] years building enterprise web applications in React, TypeScript, and Java — most recently in a senior role leading delivery across multiple client projects. I'm looking for a long-term position where I can own technical decisions, and this role looks like a strong match."

**Example (German)**:
> „Als Fullstack-Entwickler mit [X] Jahren Erfahrung in React, TypeScript und Java – zuletzt in einer Senior-Rolle mit Projektverantwortung – suche ich jetzt eine langfristige Stelle, in der ich technische Entscheidungen mitgestalten kann. Diese Ausschreibung passt gut zu meinem Profil."

**Key rules**:
- Be explicit and concrete — recruiters skim dozens of profiles. Don't bury your identity in company praise.
- Mention the specific stack or domain from the job description to confirm fit immediately.
- Avoid flattery about "the agency" — they know you're applying for the client role, not them.

## Optional/Advanced Data Collection
- **Company Culture**: Determine if formal vs. modern.
- **Visual Identity**: Identify company brand colors (for resume tailoring).
- **Recent News**: Find recent projects or product launches to reference in the "Attention" section of the cover letter.
- **Company Address**: Look up the company's "Contact" or "Impressum" page to find their official physical address so it can be added to the cover letter header.

## Step 2: Research the Company (Direct Employer only)

Before writing anything, collect these data points:

**Required — must find before writing:**
1. What does the company's product actually do? (1 sentence, in plain language)
2. Who are their target users? (individuals, enterprise, developers, etc.)
3. What problem does this specific team/role solve for the company?
4. Any recent news, launches, or product updates worth referencing?

**How to collect:**
- Web search: "[Company name] product 2025" or "[Company name] mission"
- Check their homepage, About page, and blog
- Read the JD again — the opening paragraph usually describes the product

**Output before writing:**
Summarise findings in 2-3 sentences. If you cannot find reliable information,
flag it and ask the user rather than guessing.

**Then use in cover letter:**
- Opening sentence must reference ONE specific thing from this research
- Not generic ("innovative company") — specific ("Notion's single-workspace
  approach to replacing docs, projects, and email")
- If research turns up nothing useful, use the JD's own product description
  reframed in your words
